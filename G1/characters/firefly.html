Firefly (Generation 1)

Firefly is the star of Rescue at Midnight Castle, and appears only in this special. Despite this, since she appeared in the first special of the series, Firefly is considered by many to be an iconic character. Additionally, a large amount of merchandise was based on her design and character.

General Information

Coat Light Pink
Mane Light Blue / Periwinkle
Eyes Dark Blue
Symbol / Cutie Mark 2 dark blue lightning bolts

My Thoughts

Personally, I feel that Firefly serves as a wonderful and strong protagonist and role model for viewers. While the scope and targeted audience of My Little Pony N' Friends was limited to young girls, I feel that was and still is important to portray female characters in strong, leading positions and not only relegated to supportive roles. This is a pitfall that many modern children's media still falls into today (looking at you, Paw Patrol). I feel that it is important to model to children that anyone, regardless of gender, can be a strong capable leader and a force for good. Beyond this, I feel it is an archaic practice to sort cartoons to be 'for girls' or 'for boys'. Cartoons should be for everyone.

Design-wise, I feel there isn't too much to say, except that I generally have a fondness for the way ponies look in generation 1. Firefly is no exception. Purple and Pink are my two most favorite colors and lo, Firefly has both of those.

Common Threads

According to the creator of My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic (Generation 4), firefly's personality went on to inspire the creation of the character Rainbow Dash.
